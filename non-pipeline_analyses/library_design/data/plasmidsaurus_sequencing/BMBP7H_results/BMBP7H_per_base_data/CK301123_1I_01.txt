Some customers have requested a per-base breakdown of sequencing confidence. 

There is a small chance of sequencing errors, especially the homopolymers and methylated DNA. 
The sequence GATC is methylated in E. coli by an enzyme called DAM methylase, and the sequence CCYGG 
is methylated by DCM methylase. These methylations can cause rare artifacts on our sequencing platform 
because the basecaller is not trained on mammalian or synthetic DNA that has been methylated in 
E. coli. That means certain sequences, which are underrepresented in the E. coli genomic DNA dataset, 
can have artifacts when methylated during plasmid growth in E. coli. These sequences have a characteristic 
pattern in the underlying raw data, where the two different strands give different sequences because the 
methylation site is offset on the two strands. We use that data to remove these artifacts from the consensus 
sequence, which generally works. But occasionally, things can slip through. The sequencing platform 
is also not accurate for homopolymers longer than 8 base pairs, and can occasionally have issues with 
shorter homopolymers, especially if they occur directly after a methylated GATC ['A'] or methylated CCTGG ['C'].

You can use the data below if you would like to investigate these areas yourself, and you can always 
email the expected plasmid map to support@primordiumlabs.com if you would like us to look at a specific 
unexpected region. 

Please know that our sequencing depth is greater than shown in this file, and note that the per-base 
confidence is much higher than reflected here because we perform substantial post-sequencing analysis 
to dramatically boost our confidence, as is indicated in the AB1 files.
